The Niagara of South India

Athirappilly Falls is a waterfall in Kerala, India. It's the largest waterfall in the state, standing at 81.5 feet tall. The falls are located on the Chalakudy River, which originates in the Western Ghats.

The falls are also known as the "Niagara of South India" and "Bahubali Waterfall". They're made up of four drops and fall in parallel streams. The Chalakudy River runs slowly until it reaches Athirappilly, where it picks up speed and falls over the 80-foot rock. The river remains turbulent for about a kilometer. To reach the main waterfall, visitors must walk about two kilometers from the entry gate. Some say you can spot wildlife like mountain squirrels, hornbills, sambhar deer, leopards, and elephants in the canopy.
